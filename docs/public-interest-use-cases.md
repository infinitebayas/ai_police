# Public-Interest Use Cases

This document defines safe, support-oriented use cases for AI Police.

These use cases are intentionally non-operational. They are designed to improve intake, triage, documentation, referral, and accountability.

## Design Rule

When a use case affects women, children, or people in crisis, the preferred order is:

1. identify immediate risk,
2. preserve evidence and context,
3. route to the correct human support function,
4. document decisions for review,
5. escalate only within lawful and governed pathways.

## Use Case 1: Cyberbullying Intake Support

Problem:

Children, parents, and schools often do not know whether a cyberbullying incident should be documented, reported to a platform, handled by a school, or escalated to law enforcement.

AI Police can assist by:

- turning free-text complaints into structured summaries,
- prompting evidence preservation,
- pointing users to platform reporting and school reporting pathways,
- flagging when criminal indicators may require formal escalation.

Public-interest benefit:

- faster support,
- fewer lost details,
- less confusion across schools, caregivers, and agencies.

## Use Case 2: Women’s Online Abuse Support Routing

Problem:

Women experiencing stalking, intimidation, digital abuse, or coercive control often face fragmented reporting and support routes.

AI Police can assist by:

- capturing incident context in a standardized form,
- suggesting the next lawful support channel,
- separating urgent safety concerns from non-urgent administrative follow-up,
- producing a traceable case intake record for review.

Public-interest benefit:

- lower reporting friction,
- better continuity across support services,
- more consistent handling by public-facing personnel.

## Use Case 3: Child Safety Referral Workflow

Problem:

Children at risk online may need welfare or school intervention before any formal investigative pathway.

AI Police can assist by:

- detecting that a child-centered response is needed,
- directing the case to a welfare-first queue,
- prompting role-appropriate notifications,
- preserving a limited, reviewable case trace.

Public-interest benefit:

- child-sensitive handling,
- less premature punitive escalation,
- better coordination between caregivers, schools, and public agencies.

## Use Case 4: Nodal Support Desk for India

Problem:

Large public reporting volumes and multilingual contexts can create triage bottlenecks.

AI Police can assist by:

- converting complainant narratives into structured intake notes,
- identifying probable category and urgency,
- generating multilingual guidance responses,
- routing the case toward the correct state or agency workflow for human handling.

Public-interest benefit:

- more accessible intake,
- faster routing,
- less administrative burden on nodal support teams.

## Use Case 5: School and Family Guidance Desk for the United States

Problem:

Families and schools need clearer boundaries between school discipline, mental-health support, platform reporting, and police referral.

AI Police can assist by:

- classifying incoming concerns by response type,
- generating school-safe and family-safe next-step guidance,
- identifying when a threat indicator crosses into urgent review,
- logging the recommendation path for later audit.

Public-interest benefit:

- improved consistency,
- fewer inappropriate escalations,
- clearer support pathways for adolescents.

## Use Case 6: Civilian Protection Support in Conflict-Affected Settings

Problem:

Women and children in conflict-affected settings may face violence, displacement, digital abuse, and service disruption at the same time.

AI Police can assist by:

- acting as a documentation and referral assistant,
- prompting the least harmful next step,
- routing to humanitarian or civilian protection channels,
- maintaining auditable records for supervised service coordination.

Public-interest benefit:

- reduced chaos in intake,
- improved referral consistency,
- better continuity for vulnerable civilians.

## Red Lines

AI Police should not use these use cases to justify:

- autonomous punitive action,
- mass surveillance,
- political suppression,
- identity-based targeting,
- unsupervised use by unaccountable actors.
