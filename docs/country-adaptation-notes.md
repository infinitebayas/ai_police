# Country Adaptation Notes

This document provides high-level adaptation notes for India, the United States, and conflict-affected settings.

It is not legal advice. It is a public-interest design note for lawful, support-oriented deployment planning.

## India

### India Context

- The Ministry of Home Affairs positions I4C as a national nodal point in the fight against cybercrime.
- The National Cybercrime Reporting Portal is described as having special focus on cybercrime against women and children.
- India’s scale, linguistic diversity, and federal distribution of operational responsibilities make intake and routing quality especially important.

### India Adaptation Priority

For India, AI Police should emphasize:

- multilingual intake and translation support,
- structured case routing to the correct authority,
- public nodal support with clear role controls,
- high-quality audit records for inter-state coordination,
- child and women safety pathways that reduce repeated retelling and confusion.

### India Constraints

- Do not treat AI output as a substitute for state authority.
- Do not centralize all policy judgment in a single opaque model.
- Do not bypass state/UT processes or legal review.

## United States

### United States Context

- U.S. public-health guidance recognizes meaningful youth online harm and the need for age-appropriate safety standards, stronger privacy, and better reporting processes.
- Federal cyberbullying guidance emphasizes documentation, platform reporting, school involvement, and escalation to law enforcement only for criminal conduct or serious threats.

### United States Adaptation Priority

For the U.S., AI Police should emphasize:

- school-family-agency coordination,
- youth mental-health-aware escalation,
- strong evidence documentation,
- transparent boundaries between platform reporting, school response, social support, and law-enforcement referral,
- protection for teen girls and children facing online harassment or exploitation.

### United States Constraints

- Do not collapse school discipline, mental-health triage, and criminal referral into one automated decision.
- Do not ignore state-level differences in education, privacy, and criminal law.
- Do not build punitive defaults for adolescent cases.

## Conflict-Affected and Humanitarian Settings

### Conflict Context

- Humanitarian disruption increases the risk of violence against women and children while weakening service access.
- Women and girls face elevated GBV risk, and children face compounding vulnerability when schooling, health care, and social support systems break down.

### Conflict Adaptation Priority

For conflict-affected settings, AI Police should emphasize:

- civilian protection support,
- referral-first case handling,
- low-data and low-bandwidth workflows,
- strong survivor confidentiality,
- minimal and reversible recommendations,
- compatibility with humanitarian protection standards.

### Conflict Constraints

- Do not frame the system as a battlefield or intelligence platform.
- Do not encourage coercive targeting or surveillance of civilians.
- Do not let high-risk recommendations bypass humanitarian or civilian oversight.

## Shared Adaptation Checklist

Before any country or regional rollout:

1. complete the jurisdiction matrix,
2. identify lawful operators,
3. define welfare and support referral routes,
4. define escalation approval points,
5. validate data handling rules,
6. test for disparate impact on women and children,
7. publish a local accountability and appeals process.
