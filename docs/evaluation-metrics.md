# Evaluation Metrics

AI Police should be evaluated as a public-interest support system, not only as a technical classifier.

## Safety Metrics

- False positive rate for women- and child-related cases.
- False negative rate for urgent safety referrals.
- Time to human review for high-risk cases.
- Rate of corrected or reversed recommendations.
- Rate of cases successfully routed to appropriate support channels.

## Fairness Metrics

- Differential false positive rates across gender, age, language, and displacement-related categories where lawful to assess.
- Review outcomes by region and institution.
- Escalation disparity review for vulnerable groups.

## Process Metrics

- Intake completion rate.
- Average time from report to first human response.
- Percentage of cases with complete rationale logs.
- Percentage of cases with clear final disposition.
- Appeal turnaround time.

## Public-Interest Outcome Metrics

- Reduced duplicate reporting burden.
- Increased use of correct reporting/referral channels.
- Improved school-family-agency handoff quality.
- Increased survivor access to support services where measured lawfully.
- Improved documentation quality for auditable review.

## Governance Metrics

- Percentage of releases with updated safety and privacy documentation.
- Percentage of jurisdiction profiles revalidated on schedule.
- Open critical safeguard issues.
- Number of features blocked or modified after rights or bias review.

## Conflict-Sensitive Metrics

- Percentage of conflict-setting cases handled in referral-first mode.
- Confidentiality incidents.
- Cases escalated without required human approval.
- Service continuity under degraded infrastructure.
