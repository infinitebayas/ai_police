# Note for Police and Cyber Departments

This document provides adaptation guidance for lawful public agencies evaluating AI Police.

## Purpose

AI Police provides an authoritative governance framework and configurable moderation support components. It is designed to assist institutional workflows, not replace legal process or human authority.

## Adoption Model

1. Define legal mandate and agency authority.
2. Map local offense and risk categories.
3. Configure policy and escalation tiers.
4. Enable human approval gates for consequential actions.
5. Run pilot with audit-first mode before enforcement mode.

## Minimum Governance Requirements

- Designated accountable officer.
- Legal review and compliance sign-off.
- Data protection and retention policy.
- Incident response and appeal process.
- Independent oversight or supervisory review channel.

## Public Nodal Support Layer

Where legally permitted, agencies may appoint public nodal support participants.

Recommended controls:

- role-based permissions,
- documented training,
- strict escalation boundaries,
- no unilateral punitive authority,
- periodic audits and quality checks.

## Country and Region Adaptation

Each deployment should produce a jurisdiction profile covering:

- legal basis,
- procedural safeguards,
- protected speech boundaries,
- evidence standards,
- escalation approvals,
- retention and deletion policy.

Use docs/jurisdiction-adaptation-matrix.md as the baseline template.
Use docs/country-adaptation-notes.md for high-level notes on India, the United States, and conflict-affected settings.
Use docs/social-risk-landscape.md and docs/public-interest-use-cases.md to ground deployment planning in women- and child-safety priorities.
Use docs/government-readiness.md and docs/certification-and-compliance.md to prepare internal review and hosting readiness discussions.

## Operational Caution

AI-generated recommendations can be wrong. Consequential actions should remain reviewable, attributable, and contestable.

## International Adaptation Note

Different countries have different legal frameworks for privacy, speech, cybercrime, and police powers. Configure AI Police per jurisdiction and keep legal counsel in the deployment loop.

For conflict-affected settings, keep deployment limited to civilian protection, referral support, and auditable service coordination. Do not frame the system as a coercive or intelligence platform.
